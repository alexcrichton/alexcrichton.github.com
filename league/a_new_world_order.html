A New World Order

While partisanship and perhaps stubbornness prevented the United States from becoming a full member of the League, other nations embraced it. Wilson’s dream became reality for countries ranging ranged from Canada to China to Venezuela and Japan. The League of Nations provided a forum for nations to voice their opinions as equals and resolve disputes without resorting to force. Between 1920 and 1925, the League was able to resolve a border dispute between Finland and Sweden – skirting a possible war. In 1922, the League saved Austria from economic collapse. Smaller nations, such as Albania, saw the League as a means to voice their grievances without fear of retaliation. This new medium for international cooperation was Wilson’s legacy to the world.

However, the League had its weaknesses. It lacked sufficient power to enforce controversial decisions, especially when it lacked the backing of the United States as a considerable world power. The Allies were not much help in matters that were against their interest. Circumstances in the 1930s such as the Great Depression made countries increasingly more nationalistic and protective. The liability was the imperialist ambitions of Germany, Italy, and Japan. Nazi Germany withdrew from the League in 1933, followed by Italy and Japan. With the onset of World War II, the League became marginalized to the point where any meaningful diplomacy was only conducted at the bilateral level.  It effectively ceased to function during and after World War II until it was formally dissolved by the Charter of the United Nations.

While the League of Nations ultimately failed, it was an invaluable experiment in the realm of international relations. It opened the door for a new genre of diplomacy that could be used to bring the world closer to peace. With both its strengths and failures, the League provided a model for the consequent United Nations, which derives a large part of its current structure from its predecessor. The United Nations is able to play an integral peacekeeping role in the world today by avoiding the mistakes of the League of Nations. Without the perseverance of President Woodrow Wilson, the concept of a united body of all nations would not be common knowledge today. His legacy brought the world one step closer to peace.
